Honey Orange Chicken

Servings: 4 | Prep Time: 15 min | Cook Time: 25 min

Ingredients

For the Chicken:

  • 1½ lbs boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • ½ cup cornstarch
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • Salt & pepper, to taste
  • Oil, for frying (canola or vegetable)

For the Honey Orange Sauce:

  • ½ cup Goya Sour Orange
  • ¼ cup water
  • ⅓ cup honey (plus 2–3 tbsp more to taste)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce (low sodium)
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar or apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp orange zest (optional but recommended)
  • 2–3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp grated fresh ginger (optional)
  • 1½ tbsp cornstarch + 2 tbsp water (slurry for thickening)
  • Optional: 1 tsp sesame oil or chili flakes for extra flavor

Instructions

  1. Season chicken pieces with salt and pepper. Dredge in flour, dip in egg, then coat in cornstarch.
  2. Heat 1–2 inches of oil in a skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Fry chicken in batches until golden brown and cooked through, about 4–5 minutes per batch. Drain on paper towels.
  3. In a saucepan, combine sour orange, water, honey, soy sauce, vinegar, orange zest, garlic, and ginger. Bring to a simmer over medium heat.
  4. Stir in the cornstarch slurry and continue to simmer until thickened (1–2 minutes).
  5. Toss fried chicken with the sauce in a large bowl until evenly coated. Serve immediately.

Serving Suggestions

  • Serve over steamed jasmine rice or fried rice
  • Pair with stir-fried vegetables like broccoli or snow peas
  • Garnish with green onions, sesame seeds, or orange slices
